UNSPOKEN

70%

We rely on signals beyond words. What happens when they disappear?

Research suggests that only about 30% of communication involves actual words. Around 70-80% relies on nonverbal signals such as expression, gesture, and intonation (Hull, 2016). Even if the exact percentage is debated, the conclusion is clear: words alone are never enough.

Yet in digital communication, words often become the primary channel. The majority of meaning must now travel through a narrow textual corridor.
